Mountain Ranges

Himalayan Range

The main mountain system with the highest peaks and glaciers. The Himalayan range in Jammu and Kashmir (J&K) forms a critical, high-altitude segment of the northwestern Himalaya, encompassing the Karakoram, Zanskar, and Pir Panjal ranges. Key features include glaciers, deep valleys, and peaks like Nun (7,135 m) and Kun. This region is crucial for water resources, tourism, and connectivity between Jammu and the Kashmir valley.

Location:The Himalayan range is a vast mountain system in Asia, stretching roughly 2,400 km in a west-east arc from the Indus River (Pakistan) to the Brahmaputra River (India). It acts as a natural border, separating the Indian subcontinent from the Tibetan Plateau. Wikipedia

Karakoram Range

Home to K2 and some of the world's highest mountains. The Karakoram is the world’s second-highest++ mountain range, spanning the borders of Pakistan, India, and China. Known for extreme high altitude terrain, it contains K2 (8,611m), the second-highest peak, and massive glaciers like Siachen. It is part of the Trans-Himalayas, heavily glaciated with features like the Siachen Glacier, and features the Karakoram Highway, a significant trade route. The range is a critical, heavily glaciated region forming a geopolitical junction between Central and South Asia.

Location: Primarily in the Gilgit-Baltistan region (Pakistan), Ladakh (India), and Xinjiang (China), forming part of the greater Himalayan complex alongside the Hindu Kush and Pamir mountains. Wikipedia

Pir Panjal Range

The Pir Panjal Range is a major group of mountains in the Lesser Himalayan region, stretching east-southeast to west-northwest across Himachal Pradesh and Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its high peaks, significant passes like Rohtang La and Banihal, and alpine meadows (margs), this range separates the Kashmir Valley from the Jammu region.

Location and Geography: The range runs between the Sutlej River in the east and the Beas and Ravi rivers in the west, forming a crucial part of the Himalayas. It runs across Jammu and Kashmir and Himachal Pradesh. Wikipedia

Shivalik Range

The southernmost, youngest, and lowest range of the Himalayan mountain system, running parallel to the Lesser Himalayas for over 2,400 km from the Indus River to the Brahmaputra. Also known as the Outer Himalayas, these hills span India, Nepal, and Bhutan, with average heights between 600–1,500 m and a width of 10-50 km.

Location:This range runs parallel to the main Himalayas from the Indus River in the west to the Brahmaputra in the east, spanning roughly 1,600 km to 2,400 km, covering parts of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Uttarakhand, and further east. Wikipedia

Famous Peaks

Kolahoi Peak

Mount Kolahoi (5,425m/17,799 ft), known as the "Goddess of Light," is the highest peak in the Kashmir Valley, situated in the Sonamarg region near Pahalgam. Frequently visited via the Indiahikes Kolahoi Glacier Trek, this challenging, pyramid-shaped mountain is popular for technical climbs, trekking, and exploring the rapidly receding Kolahoi Glacier.

Location and Geography: Located in the Jammu and Kashmir region of the Himalayas, it sits between the Lidder Valley and Sind Valley. It is considered the highest peak in the Kashmir division, sometimes referred to as the "Matterhorn of Kashmir"

Best Time for Trekking: May to October is the best time for trekking. Wikipedia

Harmukh Peak

Harmukh (or Haramukh) is a prominent 5,142-meter (16,870 ft) mountain in Jammu and Kashmir, known as the "Kailash of Kashmir" and a sacred site. Situated in the Ganderbal district between the Jhelum and Neelum rivers, it towers over the pristine Gangabal Lake. It is popular for steep trekking and technical climbing (best June–Sept)

Location:Situated in the Bandipora/Ganderbal districts of Jammu and Kashmir, bordering the northwestern Himalayas.

Trekking & Climbing: The peak is commonly climbed from the northwestern side of Arin Bandipore. It is known for its challenging, multi-day trekking, often starting from Narang or Arin.

Best Time to Visit: Mid-June to mid-September is considered the best time for trekking in the region. Wikipedia

Nun Kun Peaks

The Nun Kun massif is a stunning twin-peak mountain system in the Zanskar Range of the Himalayas, comprising Nun (7,135m/23,409ft) and its companion peak, Kun (7,077m/23,215ft). Situated on the Jammu & Kashmir/Ladakh border in India, this pair, separated by a 4km snowy plateau, offers a highly technical challenge for experienced climbers, requiring advanced skills for navigating crevasse-riddled, high-gradient ice walls and extreme high-altitude conditions. The best time to visit and attempt the summit is from July to September.

Location:Located in the Kargil district of India's Union Territory of Ladakh. Situated near the Suru Valley, roughly 250 km east of Srinagar.

Best Time to Climb: July to September, when the weather is relatively stable. Wikipedia
